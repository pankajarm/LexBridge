# Bayer-Monsanto Merger Timeline: What Due Diligence Missed

> This timeline reconstructs the Bayer-Monsanto $63B acquisition using the 48 documents
> in LexBridge (24 real public sources + 24 synthetic reconstructions based on public evidence).
> It demonstrates how a cross-lingual AI due diligence tool could have surfaced critical
> risks that were underestimated or overlooked before the merger closed.

## The LexBridge Thesis

All of the warning signs existed in publicly available documents **before the merger closed on June 7, 2018**:

- The IARC classification was public since **March 2015**
- The MDL 2741 consolidation of 400+ cases was public since **October 2016**
- Monsanto's 10-K disclosed 3,500 claims with **no litigation reserve** in **October 2017**
- The EPA/IARC scientific disagreement was public record
- Bellwether trial schedules were public

A cross-lingual AI due diligence system like LexBridge — combining vector search across English and German documents, knowledge graph entity linking, and automated risk analysis — could have:

1. **Surfaced the IARC classification** as a critical litigation catalyst during DD
2. **Tracked the accelerating claim count** from public MDL filings
3. **Flagged the $3B MAE threshold** as inadequate by cross-referencing claim growth trends
4. **Identified the insurance gap** ($750M vs billions in projected exposure)
5. **Bridged the language barrier** — ensuring German decision-makers had full visibility into English-language litigation documents
6. **Connected the dots** between Monsanto's "no reserve" 10-K disclosure and the simultaneously exploding claim count

The $10.9 billion settlement — and the destruction of ~40% of Bayer's market capitalization — was not unforeseeable. The evidence was there. It just wasn't connected.
